## 1. Policy Purpose and Scope

This underwriting policy establishes the standards, procedures, and authority limits for all credit decisions within Richards Credit Authority (RCA). This document governs all consumer and commercial lending activities, ensuring compliance with federal and state regulations while maintaining portfolio quality and risk-adjusted returns.

### 1.1 Applicability
- All loan officers and underwriters
- Automated underwriting systems
- Third-party originators (TPOs)
- Correspondent lending partners

### 1.2 Regulatory Framework
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
- Fair Housing Act (FHA)
- Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
- Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA)
- Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform Act
- State-specific usury and licensing laws

---

## 2. Underwriting Philosophy

RCA employs a risk-based underwriting approach that balances:
- **Prudent risk management**: Ensuring borrower ability to repay
- **Fair access to credit**: Prohibiting discriminatory practices
- **Operational efficiency**: Leveraging technology for consistent decisions
- **Regulatory compliance**: Meeting all legal and regulatory requirements

---

## 3. Authority Structure

### 3.1 Underwriter Authority Levels

| Level | Designation | Max Loan Amount | Max LTV | Exceptions Allowed |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|---------|-------------------|
| I | Junior Underwriter | $250,000 | 80% | None |
| II | Underwriter | $500,000 | 90% | 1 per file |
| III | Senior Underwriter | $1,000,000 | 95% | 2 per file |
| IV | Lead Underwriter | $2,000,000 | 97% | 3 per file |
| V | Chief Underwriter | Unlimited | 100% | Unlimited (with documentation) |

### 3.2 Dual Verification Requirements
All loans exceeding $1,000,000 or with LTV >90% require dual verification:
- Primary underwriter assessment
- Secondary underwriter review
- Final sign-off by Senior Underwriter minimum

---

## 4. Credit Analysis Standards

### 4.1 Credit History Evaluation

**Minimum Requirements:**
- 2-year credit history with at least 3 tradelines
- No bankruptcies within 24 months (Chapter 7) or 12 months (Chapter 13 discharge)
- No foreclosures within 36 months
- No open collections >$5,000 unless payment plan documented

**Extenuating Circumstances:**
Documented events allowing exception to waiting periods:
- Death of primary wage earner
- Serious illness with medical documentation
- Divorce with legal documentation
- Job loss with subsequent re-employment

### 4.2 Capacity Analysis

**Debt-to-Income (DTI) Calculation:**
```

Total Monthly Debt Payments / Gross Monthly Income = DTI Ratio

```

**Maximum DTI by Loan Type:**
- Conventional: 43% (up to 50% with compensating factors)
- FHA: 43% (up to 50% with automated underwriting approval)
- VA: 41% (residual income analysis required above 41%)
- Jumbo: 36% (strict enforcement)

**Compensating Factors (when DTI exceeds standard):**
- 12 months reserves after closing
- 20% down payment
- 5+ years stable employment
- Credit score >740
- No housing payment increase

### 4.3 Collateral Assessment

**Appraisal Requirements:**
- Full appraisal for all purchase transactions >$250,000
- Drive-by appraisal acceptable for refinances <80% LTV
- Automated valuation models (AVMs) permitted for refinances <75% LTV and <$400,000
- 2nd appraisal required for:
  - Loan amounts >$1,500,000
  - Properties in declining markets
  - Cash-out refinances >$500,000

**Property Standards:**
- Must meet minimum property requirements (MPR) for FHA/VA
- No unpermitted additions without local authority verification
- Environmental hazards must be remediated or insured

---

## 5. Documentation Requirements

### 5.1 Full Documentation (Full Doc)
Required for:
- Self-employed borrowers
- Jumbo loans
- Investment properties
- Any loan with risk layering

**Income Documentation:**
- W-2s for past 2 years
- Pay stubs covering most recent 30 days
- IRS Form 4506-C (transcript request)
- Business tax returns (if self-employed, 2 years)

**Asset Documentation:**
- 2 months bank statements (all accounts)
- Investment account statements
- Gift letter with donor documentation (if applicable)

### 5.2 Alternative Documentation (Alt Doc)
Available for:
- W-2 employees with stable employment
- Credit scores >700
- Primary residences
- LTV <80%

**Acceptable Forms:**
- 12 months bank statements (income calculated via deposits)
- 1099 forms for independent contractors
- Asset depletion method for high-net-worth individuals

### 5.3 Reduced Documentation
- **Streamlined**: Available for existing customers with 12+ month payment history
- **No Ratio**: Investment properties only, heavy reliance on collateral
- **Stated Income**: Prohibited per regulatory guidance

---

## 6. Risk Layering and Mitigation

### 6.1 Risk Factors Matrix

| Factor | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk |
|--------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Credit Score | >720 | 640-719 | <640 |
| DTI | <36% | 36-43% | >43% |
| LTV | <80% | 80-90% | >90% |
| Reserves | >12 months | 6-12 months | <6 months |
| Property Type | SFR | Condo | Multi-family |
| Loan Purpose | Purchase | Rate/Term Refi | Cash-out Refi |

**Risk Layering Limits:**
- No more than 2 "High Risk" factors permitted
- 3+ "High Risk" factors = automatic decline
- Any "High Risk" factor requires compensating factor

### 6.2 Fraud Prevention

**Red Flags Requiring Investigation:**
- Inconsistent signatures across documents
- Round-number income figures
- Recent opening of multiple credit accounts
- Appraisal value significantly above comparable sales
- Borrower reluctant to provide documentation

**Verification Protocols:**
- Employment verification via independent source
- IRS transcript verification for all income documentation
- Social Security number validation
- Occupancy verification for primary residence claims

---

## 7. Decision Documentation

### 7.1 Approval Conditions

All approvals must include:
- List of prior-to-document (PTD) conditions
- List of prior-to-funding (PTF) conditions
- Explanation of any guideline exceptions
- Compensating factors documented
- Risk rating assignment

### 7.2 Denial Requirements

Adverse action notices must include:
- Specific reason for denial (ECOA compliance)
- Credit bureau used (if applicable)
- Notice of right to obtain free credit report
- Appeal process information

### 7.3 Counter-Offers

When counter-offering:
- Document specific changes required for approval
- Provide 30-day acceptance window
- Maintain original application data
- Require re-underwriting if terms change significantly

---

## 8. Quality Control

### 8.1 Pre-Funding QC
- 10% random sample review
- 100% review of loans with exceptions
- 100% review of loans >$1,000,000
- Turnaround time: 24 hours maximum

### 8.2 Post-Close QC
- Monthly random sample of 10% of funded loans
- Re-underwriting to original standards
- Collateral verification
- Documentation completeness audit

### 8.3 Defect Rating
- **Critical**: Material misrepresentation or fraud
- **Major**: Significant guideline deviation without approval
- **Minor**: Documentation omission or clerical error
- **Informational**: Best practice recommendation

---

## 9. Policy Exceptions

### 9.1 Exception Authority
Exceptions to policy require:
- Written justification
- Compensating factors documentation
- Next-level supervisor approval
- Tracking in exception database

### 9.2 Prohibited Exceptions
- No exceptions permitted on:
  - Fair lending compliance
  - Maximum legal interest rates
  - Required federal disclosures
  - Flood insurance requirements

---

## 10. Policy Maintenance

### 10.1 Review Cycle
- Quarterly review of credit criteria
- Annual comprehensive policy review
- Ad hoc updates for regulatory changes

### 10.2 Change Control
All policy changes require:
- Risk committee approval
- Legal compliance review
- Training material updates
- System configuration updates
- 30-day advance notice (unless regulatory emergency)
