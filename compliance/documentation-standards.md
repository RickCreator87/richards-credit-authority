# Richards Credit Authority - Documentation Standards

**Document ID:** RCA-COMP-DOC-001  
**Version:** 2.0.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the minimum documentation standards for all lending activities at Richards Credit Authority (RCA). Proper documentation ensures regulatory compliance, legal enforceability, audit readiness, and operational efficiency.

---

## 2. Regulatory Framework

Documentation requirements derive from:
- **ECOA (Regulation B)**: Application and adverse action records
- **TILA (Regulation Z)**: Disclosure and billing statements
- **RESPA**: Closing documents and escrow records
- **FCRA**: Credit report usage and disclosures
- **GLBA**: Privacy notices and information security
- **HMDA**: Application and loan data recording
- **TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosures)**: Loan estimate and closing disclosure
- **State Lending Laws**: Varying documentation requirements

---

## 3. Document Retention

### 3.1 Retention Periods

| Document Category | Retention Period | Format | Location |
|-------------------|------------------|--------|----------|
| Loan Applications | 25 years | Electronic | Document Management System |
| Credit Decisions | 25 years | Electronic | DMS |
| Closing Documents | Life of loan + 7 years | Electronic + Microfilm | DMS + Offsite Vault |
| Servicing Records | Life of loan + 7 years | Electronic | DMS |
| Bankruptcy Records | Life of loan + 7 years | Electronic + Physical | DMS + Offsite |
| Foreclosure Records | Life of loan + 7 years | Electronic + Physical | DMS + Offsite |
| HMDA LAR | 3 years | Electronic | Compliance System |
| Adverse Action Notices | 25 years | Electronic | DMS |
| Privacy Notices | Life of relationship | Electronic | DMS |
| Communication Logs | 7 years | Electronic | CRM System |

### 3.2 Format Requirements

**Electronic Documents**:
- PDF/A format for archival (ISO 19005 standard)
- Minimum 300 DPI for scanned documents
- OCR text layer for searchability
- Digital signatures where original signature required
- Metadata preservation (creation date, author, version)

**Physical Documents**:
- Acid-free paper for original documents
- Fire-resistant storage for negotiable instruments
- Climate-controlled environment (65°F, 35% humidity)
- Access logging for all retrievals

---

## 4. Application Documentation

### 4.1 Required Application Documents

**Universal Residential Loan Application (URLA)**:
- Fannie Mae Form 1003 / Freddie Mac Form 65
- All fields completed (no blanks)
- Borrower and co-borrower signatures
- Date of application
- Loan officer identification

**Supporting Documentation**:

| Category | Documents | Verification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Identity | Government-issued photo ID | Visual verification + copy |
| Income | W-2s, pay stubs, tax returns | IRS transcript match |
| Assets | Bank statements, investment accounts | Direct source verification |
| Employment | Verification of employment | Written or verbal VOE |
| Credit | Credit report | Tri-merge report |
| Property | Appraisal, title commitment | Independent third party |

### 4.2 Document Quality Standards

**Completeness**:
- All pages present (including blank pages)
- All signatures obtained
- All dates completed
- No white-out or erasures (cross-outs initialed)

**Legibility**:
- Minimum 10-point font for disclosures
- Contrast ratio 4.5:1 for text
- No smudging or fading
- Complete visibility of all security features

**Accuracy**:
- Consistent information across documents
- Mathematical accuracy on calculations
- Correct legal descriptions
- Accurate personal information

---

## 5. Disclosure Documentation

### 5.1 Timing Requirements

| Disclosure | When Required | Delivery Deadline |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Loan Estimate (LE) | Application | Within 3 business days |
| Closing Disclosure (CD) | Before closing | At least 3 business days prior |
| Initial ARM Disclosure | ARM applications | With application |
| CHARM Booklet | ARM applications | With application |
| Servicing Disclosure | Application | Within 3 business days |
| Privacy Notice | Application | At application |
| Affiliated Business Arrangement | Referral involved | At time of referral |
| Escrow Closing Notice | Escrow closing | At least 3 business days prior |

### 5.2 Disclosure Content Standards

**Loan Estimate**:
- All required fields completed
- Accurate fees (0% tolerance for lender fees, 10% tolerance for third-party)
- Correct APR and TIP calculations
- Property address or identification
- Signature not required but delivery documented

**Closing Disclosure**:
- Side-by-side comparison with LE
- Explanation of changes exceeding tolerance thresholds
- Borrower acknowledgment signature
- Creditor signature
- Settlement agent information

- Witness signatures where required by state law

**Notarization**:

# Richards Credit Authority - Record Retention Policy

**Document ID:** RCA-COMP-RET-001  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer  
**Legal Review:** General Counsel

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for retention, storage, and destruction of records at Richards Credit Authority (RCA) to ensure compliance with federal and state regulations, support business operations, protect legal rights, and manage litigation risks.

---

## 2. Regulatory Basis

Retention periods are determined by requirements from:
- **Federal Deposit Insurance Act**: 5 years
- **Bank Secrecy Act**: 5 years
- **Truth in Lending Act**: 2 years (advertising), 25 years (closed loans)
- **Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act**: 5 years
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act**: 25 months (applications), 25 years (closed loans)
- **Home Mortgage Disclosure Act**: 3 years
- **Fair Credit Reporting Act**: 7 years (credit information)
- **Internal Revenue Code**: 7 years (tax records)
- **State Statutes of Limitations**: Varies (contract, fraud, property)
- **SEC Regulations**: 7 years (if applicable)

---

## 5. Storage Requirements

### 5.1 Electronic Storage

**Primary Storage (Active)**:
- High-availability storage (99.9% uptime)
- Daily backups
- 7-year online retention
- Full-text search capability
- Access controls and audit logging

**Secondary Storage (Archival)**:
- Lower-cost storage tier
- Monthly backups
- 8-25 year retention
- Retrieval within 24 hours
- Compression and deduplication

**Tertiary Storage (Deep Archive)**:
- Tape or cloud archive
- Annual backups
- Permanent retention (if required)
- Retrieval within 72 hours
- Encryption at rest

### 5.2 Physical Storage

**Onsite Storage**:
- Fire-resistant filing cabinets
- Limited to active files (<2 years)
- Access controls
- Climate control (65-75°F, 35-45% humidity)

**Offsite Storage**:
- Commercial records center
- Climate-controlled vault
- Barcode tracking
- Retrieval service (same day or next day)
- Fire suppression and security

**Vault Storage**:
- Original promissory notes
- Original recorded mortgages
- Irreplaceable documents
- Biometric access controls
- 24/7 monitoring

### 5.3 Storage Standards

**Environmental Controls**:
- Temperature: 65°F ± 5°F
- Humidity: 35% ± 5%
- Light exposure: Minimal (UV filtering)
- Air quality: Filtered, pollutant-free

**Security Controls**:
- Access logging
- Background checks for personnel
- Encryption for electronic
- Locked containers for transport

## 6. Legal Hold Procedures

### 6.1 Triggering Events

Legal hold (litigation hold) is required upon:
- Receipt of subpoena or court order
- Notice of pending litigation
- Regulatory investigation
- Threat of legal action
- Significant complaint or dispute
- Bankruptcy filing by borrower

### 6.2 Hold Implementation

**Immediate Actions (Within 24 hours)**:
1. Notify Records Management of hold
2. Suspend routine destruction
3. Identify custodians
4. Preserve relevant systems
5. Document hold implementation

**Hold Notice Contents**:
- Reason for hold
- Scope of preservation
- Relevant date ranges
- Types of records to preserve
- Custodian responsibilities
- Contact for questions

### 6.3 Hold Management

**Ongoing Obligations**:
- Quarterly hold reminders
- New custodian notification
- Scope modification as case evolves
- Hold release upon resolution

**Documentation**:
- Hold notice distribution list
- Acknowledgments from custodians
- Preservation actions taken
- Hold release authorization

---

**Approval Requirements**:

- Compliance verification (for regulated records)

**Electronic Records**:
- NIST 800-88 compliant wiping
- Cryptographic erasure
- Physical destruction of media (if retired)
- Certificate of destruction
